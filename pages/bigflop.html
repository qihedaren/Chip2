The Ultimate Failed Chips

These products earned their place in the Hall of Shame through spectacular failures, odd marketing decisions, or memorably bad taste.

WOW! Chips (1998)

The infamous olestra chips that promised fat-free snacking but delivered unexpected and embarrassing side effects. The FDA required these chips to carry a warning label about "anal leakage" and other digestive issues.

Despite initial strong sales, consumer complaints and jokes on late-night TV led to their demise. Frito-Lay eventually rebranded them as "Light" chips with a reformulated recipe.

Why it failed: Medical side effects, negative publicity, and becoming the butt of jokes (pun intended).

Hall of Shame Votes: 256

Lay's Limon (2002)

Lay's attempt to capture the Hispanic market with a lime-flavored chip that missed the mark entirely. Instead of the authentic lime flavor consumers expected, these chips tasted intensely artificial and overly sour.

Despite heavy marketing, they quickly disappeared from shelves. The failure was particularly notable because lime-flavored chips from smaller brands were successful at the same time.

Why it failed: Poor flavor execution, artificial taste, and failing to understand the target market's preference for authentic flavors.
